# Physical Fitness Test (PFT) Procedures

## 1. Purpose

This SOP describes the procedures for administering and participating in the AFROTC Physical Fitness Test at Det 999.

## 2. Test Components

The PFT consists of three events conducted in the following order:

1. **Push-ups** - one minute, two-count cadence.
2. **Sit-ups** - one minute.
3. **1.5-mile run** - timed, conducted on the track at Midstate University Memorial Stadium.

Cadets are scored according to the current AFROTCMAN scoring tables based on age and gender.

## 3. Scheduling

The PFT is administered twice per semester. Dates are published in the semester OPORD (`opord_001_fall_semester_2025`). Cadets must sign up via the PT signup form posted in the #pt-prep channel no later than 72 hours before the test.

## 4. Pre-Test Requirements

- Cadets must have a current medical clearance on file with the Det.
- Cadets on a Medical Hold cannot participate; see Capt Brian Olsen for status.
- PT uniform per `sop_001_uniform_standards` section 3.3.
- Hydration is the cadet's responsibility. Water will be available at the stadium.

## 5. Test-Day Procedure

- Report time: 0630 unless otherwise specified in the OPORD.
- Sign in with the testing NCO (TSgt Michael Romano).
- Warm-up led by the senior cadet on station.
- Events conducted in order with no more than 10-minute breaks between events.
- Scoring NCO records results on the standard scoresheet.

## 6. Failure and Retest

Cadets who do not achieve a passing composite score will be placed on a PT improvement plan with the Standards & Evaluations officer (C/Capt Taylor Brooks). Retests occur within 30 days.

## 7. Common Issues

Recurring problems documented in AARs include signup confusion (cadets unsure where to sign up), late arrival, and inadequate hydration. Flight CCs are responsible for ensuring their flights know the schedule.

## 8. Point of Contact

C/Capt Drew Larson, S3 / Plans, owns PFT scheduling. TSgt Michael Romano administers the test.
