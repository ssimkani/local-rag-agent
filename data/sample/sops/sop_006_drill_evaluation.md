# Drill and Ceremonies Evaluation

## 1. Purpose

This SOP defines drill evaluation criteria and the procedures used during LLAB and Warrior Day to assess flight drill proficiency.

## 2. Authority

Drill conducted per AFMAN 36-2203, with Det 999-specific clarifications below.

## 3. Required Movements

Flights must be proficient in:

- Fall in, fall out, dismissal.
- Right face, left face, about face.
- Forward march, halt.
- Column right, column left.
- Mark time.
- Eyes right and present arms (for ceremonies).

AS300 and AS400 cadets are additionally responsible for command voice and flight leader procedures.

## 4. Evaluation Format

- Evaluations occur during a designated LLAB and at Warrior Day.
- Each flight presents for 10 minutes.
- Evaluators (Cadre and senior cadets) score on: precision, command voice, uniformity, response to commands, recovery from errors.

## 5. Scoring Rubric

A four-point scale per element:

- 4 - Exemplary
- 3 - Proficient
- 2 - Needs improvement
- 1 - Unsatisfactory

A flight must achieve an overall score of 3.0 or higher to be considered proficient for the semester.

## 6. Flight Drill Practice

Flights are expected to conduct independent drill practice at least twice per week outside of LLAB. Flight CCs coordinate practice times.

## 7. Recurring Issues

Per AARs from prior semesters:

- Flights underestimate the rehearsal time needed.
- Command voice is a recurring weak point, especially for first-time flight leaders.
- Recovery from a missed command tends to cascade; flights should rehearse the recovery itself.

## 8. Point of Contact

C/Capt Drew Larson (S3) coordinates drill evaluations. Flight CCs are responsible for their flight's preparation.
